# United States Senate

The **United States Senate** is the upper chamber of the U.S. Congress.  
It is composed of **100 Senators**, two from each state, serving staggered six-year terms. Senators represent the interests of their entire state in national legislation and play a key role in approving federal appointments and treaties.

## ğŸ“˜ About the Senate

- Each Senator serves a **six-year term**, with elections staggered so that approximately one-third of the seats are up for election every two years.
- The Senate has the **exclusive power to confirm** presidential appointments (such as judges, cabinet members, and ambassadors).
- The Senate also has the **sole power to ratify treaties** and to conduct **impeachment trials**.
